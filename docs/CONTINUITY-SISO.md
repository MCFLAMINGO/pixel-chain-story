# Come Into the Light — no parallel Facebook

## The ICP-shaped trap

On some platforms you must **rebuild the product inside their world** to have
the product there: a calculator *on ICP* means writing an ICP calculator.
That becomes two Facebooks — one on AWS, one rewritten for the platform.

## Pixel rule

**Build anywhere, in any language.**  
When you **come into the light**, Pixel accepts the artifact by content digest
and continuity mirrors — not by forcing a Pixel VM rewrite.

| | Parallel-build platforms | Pixel Ledger + SISO |
|---|---|---|
| Calculator | Rewrite for their VM | Keep your code; shine in the digest + mirrors |
| Facebook | Second codebase | One codebase; continuity if origin dies |
| Languages | Often constrained | Any — TS, Python, Rust, Go, Java, … |
| Host | Their subnet or nothing | AWS today, peers tomorrow |

## SISO

- **Shine In** — register / mirror / attest foreign apps, MCP agents, containers into Pixel light  
- **Shine Out** — carry Pixel proofs or wake mirrors on other networks  

If AWS goes down and the service **believed in Pixel** (illuminated + mirrors),
peers can still serve it. That is continuity by light — not a second product.

## What you register

A language-agnostic `LightArtifact`: name, kind, digest, languages[], origin host, mirrors[].

States: `outside` → `in_superposition` → `in_the_light` → (optional) `origin_dark`.
